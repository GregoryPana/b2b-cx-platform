# Installation Assessment Survey User Guide

This guide explains the Installation Assessment Survey in simple, non-technical language for a new user.

This system is used to record the quality of an installation job by completing a set of scored checks. The system guides you through the process, but it is still important to work carefully and review your answers before submitting.

---

## 1) What This System Is For

The Installation Assessment Survey helps you:

- record installation details
- confirm who completed the work
- score the installation against the checklist
- save unfinished work as a draft
- submit the final assessment when complete

It is best to think of this system as a digital inspection form.

---

## 2) Before You Start

Before opening the assessment, try to have the following ready:

- the work order number
- customer name
- location details
- date work was done
- whether the job was done by **Field Team** or **Contractor**
- contractor name or field team member names if required

This will make the form easier to complete without going back and forth.

---

## 3) Getting Access

### Step by step

1. Open the Installation Assessment Survey link.
2. If a security warning appears, click **Advanced**.
3. Click **Proceed to site** or **Continue to website**.
4. Sign in with your work account.
5. Wait for the page to load.
6. Confirm the assessment page is visible.

### How you know you are in the right place

You should see:

- the left menu
- the assessment page in the main area
- the scoring guide on screen

### Important notes

- If the page does not load correctly, refresh once.
- If you cannot access the page, contact your administrator.

## 4) Simple Overall Flow

For most users, the normal process is:

1. choose **New** or **Draft**
2. complete the assessment details
3. confirm worker information
4. score each question from 1 to 5
5. use progress to check what is missing
6. save a draft or submit the finished assessment

Following this order helps prevent missed steps.

---

## 5) Page: Start (New or Draft)

This is where you decide whether you are beginning a new assessment or returning to work you already saved.

### What this page is for

- starting fresh
- reopening unfinished work

### Step by step

1. Choose **New** to start a new assessment.
2. Choose **Draft** to continue existing work.
3. If using **Draft**, select the correct saved draft.

### How to decide

- choose **New** if this job has not been started before
- choose **Draft** if you already entered some information and want to continue

### Important warning

If you choose the wrong draft, you may continue the wrong assessment. Always check carefully before proceeding.

### What you can do

- start a new assessment
- continue saved work

### What you cannot do

- continue a draft without selecting it
- assume the system will guess the correct draft for you

## 6) Page: Assessment Details

This section tells the system what installation job you are scoring.

### What this section is for

- linking the assessment to the correct job
- recording customer and site information
- capturing who completed the work

### Step by step

1. Enter the quality assurance inspector name.
2. Enter the work order.
3. Enter the customer name.
4. Enter the location.
5. Enter the date work was done.
6. Select the customer type.
7. Select whether the job was done by **Field Team** or **Contractor**.

### Worker details

If **Contractor** is selected:

- choose the contractor name from the available list

If **Field Team** is selected:

- enter the names of the field team members
- you can add up to 5 names

### Why this matters

These details are used later in reporting and analytics. If the wrong worker details are entered, the results may appear under the wrong team or contractor.

## 7) Page: Scoring The Assessment

This is the main working area where you inspect and score the job.

### How scoring works

Each question is scored from **1 to 5**.

In simple terms:

- **1** means very poor or serious problem
- **5** means very good or fully acceptable

The system uses your answers to calculate the overall assessment result.

### Step by step

1. Open the first category.
2. Read the question fully.
3. Select a score from **1 to 5**.
4. Move to the next question.
5. Continue until the whole category is complete.
6. Move to the next category.
7. Repeat until all categories are finished.

### Understanding categories

Categories group similar checks together. This makes the work easier to review and helps you stay organized.

### Understanding progress

The progress display helps you see:

- how much of the assessment is complete
- whether anything is missing
- whether you are ready to save or submit

### What you can do

- score each question clearly
- work through the inspection in order
- use the on-screen progress to avoid missing items

### What you cannot do

- use scores outside 1 to 5
- submit if required items are incomplete

## 8) Saving A Draft

Use **Save Draft** if you are not yet ready to submit.

### When to use it

- you are still checking details
- you need to confirm information with someone else
- you need to stop and continue later

### Why this matters

Saving a draft protects your work. If you leave the page without saving, unfinished answers may be lost.

**Image:**

![Save Draft Button](./images/survey-installation-assessment/save-draft.png)

---

## 9) Deleting A Draft

Use **Delete Draft** only when you are sure that draft should no longer be used.

### When to use it

- the draft was created by mistake
- the draft is no longer needed
- the draft should be restarted from the beginning

### Important warning

Deleting a draft removes your unfinished work. Do not delete it unless you are sure.

**Image:**

![Delete Draft Button](./images/survey-installation-assessment/delete-draft.png)

---

## 10) Submitting The Assessment

Use **Submit** only when the assessment is complete and correct.

### Before submitting

Check that:

- all details are correct
- the worker information is correct
- all required questions have a score
- progress shows completion

### What happens after submit

The assessment is treated as a completed record and can be used in dashboard reporting and review.

### Good habit

Pause for a final read-through before clicking submit.

**Image:**

![Submit Button](./images/survey-installation-assessment/submit-button.png)

## 11) Navigation Tips

Use the left menu to move between:

- **Assessment**
- **User Guide**

If you feel unsure, open the **User Guide** page without leaving the system.

---

## 12) Common Mistakes To Avoid

- entering the wrong work order
- selecting the wrong worker type
- forgetting to add contractor or field team names
- leaving unfinished work without saving
- submitting before reviewing the answers

---

## 13) Common Situations

- **Situation:** The date field shows an error.
  - **What to do:** Re-enter the date using the date picker.

- **Situation:** The score does not look selected.
  - **What to do:** Click the score again and confirm the selection is visible.

- **Situation:** A draft warning appears.
  - **What to do:** Check whether the work was already started and continue the draft if needed.

- **Situation:** The contractor name is not available.
  - **What to do:** Stop and ask the dashboard administrator to add the contractor to the list.

---

## 14) Getting Help

If something goes wrong:

1. take a screenshot

3. note what you were trying to do
4. send the details to your administrator or supervisor
