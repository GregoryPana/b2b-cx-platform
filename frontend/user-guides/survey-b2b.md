# B2B Survey User Guide

This guide explains the B2B Survey in clear, simple language for new users.

You do not need technical knowledge to use this system. The most important thing is to work slowly, read each label on screen, and save your work if you are not ready to submit.

---

## 1) What This System Is For

The B2B Survey system is used to record customer visit information and complete survey questions for a business visit.

You will mainly use it to:

- open a planned visit
- complete survey details
- answer questions category by category
- save a draft if you need to stop
- submit the survey when it is complete

Think of it as a guided form that helps you capture the visit properly from start to finish.

---

## 2) Before You Start

Before opening a survey, make sure you have:

- the correct business or site
- the correct visit date
- the correct person or team details if needed
- enough information to answer the questions honestly

If you are unsure which visit to use, check with your supervisor before starting.

---

## 3) Getting Access

### Step by step

1. Open the B2B Survey link.
2. If a security warning appears, click **Advanced**.
3. Click **Proceed to site** or **Continue to website**.
4. Sign in with your work account.
5. Wait for the page to load fully.
6. Confirm you can see the left-side menu and the survey workspace.

### How you know you are in the right place

You should be able to see:

- a left menu with pages such as **Planned Visits**, **Survey**, and **User Guide**
- the main working area in the center of the screen
- your account shown in the system header or side area

### Important notes

- If the page looks blank, refresh once.
- If you cannot sign in, stop and contact your administrator.
- Do not use another person’s account to complete a survey.

## 4) Simple Overall Flow

For most users, the normal process is:

1. Open **Planned Visits**
2. Choose the correct visit
3. Open the survey
4. Decide if you are starting **New** or continuing a **Draft**
5. Complete the survey details at the top
6. Answer questions in each category
7. Check progress before finishing
8. Click **Save Draft** if not finished, or **Submit** if complete

If you follow this order, the system will feel much easier to use.

---

## 5) Page: Planned Visits

**Menu icon:** Calendar icon (`CalendarDays`)

This page helps you find the visit you are supposed to work on.

### What this page is for

- finding the correct planned visit
- checking the visit status before starting
- opening the survey from the correct record

### What to do on this page

1. Open **Planned Visits** from the left menu.
2. Look through the visit list carefully.
3. Check the business name, date, and any other visit information.
4. Select the correct visit.
5. Open the survey from that visit.

### Good habit

Always double-check the business and date before opening a survey. This helps prevent capturing answers under the wrong visit.

### What you can do

- choose the correct visit to work on
- confirm you are using the right record

### What you cannot do

- you cannot finish and submit the full survey from this page
- you should not use this page to guess which visit belongs to you

## 6) Page: Survey Workspace

**Menu icon:** Clipboard / survey icon (`ClipboardCheck`)

This is the main page where the real work happens.

### What this page is for

- starting a new survey
- reopening an unfinished draft
- entering top-level visit details
- answering survey questions
- tracking progress to completion

### Main areas on this page

The survey workspace normally includes:

- **New / Draft selector**
- **Survey details section**
- **Category question area**
- **Progress tracker**
- **Save Draft button**
- **Submit button**

### Step by step

1. Choose **New** if this is the first time you are completing the survey.
2. Choose **Draft** if you already started and want to continue.
3. If using **Draft**, select the correct saved draft.
4. Complete the details section at the top of the page.
5. Open the first category.
6. Answer every required question carefully.
7. Move to the next category.
8. Repeat until all categories are complete.

### How to work safely

- read each question fully before answering
- do not rush through a category
- if extra explanation is needed, add notes where the form allows it
- if you need to stop, save before closing the page

### Understanding the details section

The details section is important because it tells the system which visit the answers belong to.

If these details are wrong, the survey may still save, but the data may be attached to the wrong visit or become difficult to review later.

### Understanding categories

Questions are grouped into categories so that related questions stay together.

This makes the survey easier to read. It also helps you work in a structured way instead of jumping around the form.

### Understanding progress

The progress tracker shows how much of the survey is complete.

Use it to:

- see if anything is missing
- find unfinished work
- check whether you are ready to submit

### What you can do

- create a new survey response
- continue from a saved draft
- answer questions in stages
- track your completion as you work

### What you cannot do

- skip required fields and still submit
- rely on the browser to keep unsaved work
- submit a survey if important information is still missing

## 7) Saving A Draft

Use **Save Draft** when you are not ready to finish.

### When you should save a draft

- you need to leave the page and return later
- you are waiting for missing information
- you want to stop and review answers before final submission

### Why this matters

Saving a draft protects your work. If you close the browser without saving, unsaved answers can be lost.

### Good habit

If you have spent more than a few minutes entering answers, save before taking a break.

**Image:**

![Review and Save/Submit - Save Draft Button](./images/survey-b2b/save-draft-button.png)

## 8) Submitting The Survey

Use **Submit** only when the survey is complete and you are satisfied with the answers.

### Before you click Submit

Check that:

- the visit details are correct
- all required questions are answered
- notes are added where needed
- the progress tracker shows the survey is complete

### What happens after submit

After submission, the survey moves forward in the workflow for review.

That means your answers may be checked by someone else before they are treated as final reporting data.

### Important warning

Do not submit just to “finish later.”

If you are not finished, save a draft instead.

## 9) Navigation Tips

### Left menu

Use the left menu to move between pages.

For normal work, the most important pages are:

- **Planned Visits**
- **Survey**
- **User Guide**

### Menu icons to help you identify pages

- **Planned Visits**: calendar icon
- **Survey**: clipboard / survey icon
- **User Guide**: book icon

### Best way to move around

- start in **Planned Visits**
- work in **Survey**
- open **User Guide** if you need help

Avoid opening many tabs at the same time unless you really need them. This makes it easier to keep track of your work.

---

## 10) Common Mistakes To Avoid

- opening the wrong visit
- forgetting to save before leaving
- submitting while required questions are still missing
- choosing the wrong draft
- answering too quickly without reading the question properly

If you are unsure, stop and check before continuing.

---

## 11) Common Situations

- **Situation:** Your draft is not listed.
  - **What to do:** Switch to **Draft** mode and check again carefully.

- **Situation:** You need to stop now.
  - **What to do:** Click **Save Draft** before leaving the page.

- **Situation:** A question is unclear.
  - **What to do:** Add a short note if needed, then check with your supervisor.

- **Situation:** You think you opened the wrong visit.
  - **What to do:** Stop immediately, go back to Planned Visits, and open the correct record.

---

## 12) Getting Help

If something goes wrong:

1. take a screenshot
2. write down what page you were on
3. write down what you were trying to do
4. send that information to your administrator or supervisor

This makes it much easier for someone to help you quickly.
